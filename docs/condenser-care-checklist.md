# Condenser Care Checklist: Quarterly Inspection

Use this checklist every three months to keep your outdoor AC unit in top condition. Print it out or save it to your phone.

## Safety First
- [ ] Turn off AC at thermostat
- [ ] Turn off power at the outdoor disconnect switch
- [ ] Wait 5 minutes for capacitors to discharge

## Visual Inspection
- [ ] Check for visible damage to the housing or fins
- [ ] Look for oil stains around the base (refrigerant leak indicator)
- [ ] Inspect electrical wiring for fraying, corrosion, or pest damage
- [ ] Verify the unit sits level on its pad
- [ ] Check the disconnect box for corrosion, water, or ant nests

## Airflow and Clearance
- [ ] Trim vegetation to 24 inches clearance on all sides
- [ ] Remove leaves, branches, and debris from around the unit
- [ ] Clear any debris from the top grille
- [ ] Verify nothing is stacked on or leaning against the unit

## Coil Cleaning
- [ ] Spray coils from inside-out with garden hose
- [ ] Work around all four sides
- [ ] Rinse until water runs clear
- [ ] Do NOT use a pressure washer
- [ ] Allow to dry before restoring power

## Drainage
- [ ] Check that the area around the pad drains properly
- [ ] Clear any standing water
- [ ] Verify the condensate drain line from the indoor unit is flowing (check the outdoor drip point)

## Restore Power
- [ ] Turn on disconnect switch
- [ ] Turn on AC at thermostat
- [ ] Listen for normal startup sounds (fan + compressor engaging)
- [ ] Verify cool air is coming from vents within 5 minutes

## Red Flags — Call a Professional
If you observe any of these, schedule a [professional inspection](https://ac-repair.today/services/ac-maintenance/):

- [ ] Ice on refrigerant lines
- [ ] Compressor does not start (fan runs alone)
- [ ] Grinding, screeching, or loud buzzing sounds
- [ ] Unit vibrates excessively
- [ ] Oil stains near the base
- [ ] Burn smell from the unit or disconnect box
- [ ] System runs but home does not cool
